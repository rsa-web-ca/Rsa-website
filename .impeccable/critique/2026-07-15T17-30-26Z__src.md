## Anti-Patterns Verdict

**LLM assessment**: Does not read as AI slop. Semantic OKLCH tokens, a serif/sans pairing chosen for the register (Frank Ruhl Libre + Public Sans), border-top service list instead of icon-card grids, one kicker on the hero only. The weakest tell is the hero image: an anonymous glass skyscraper — exactly the "big-four gloss" the PRODUCT.md anti-references.

**Deterministic scan**: 1 finding — `side-tab` on `src/index.css:219` (`border-left: 3px solid var(--accent)` on `.blog-prose blockquote`). False positive: a left rule on a blockquote is the conventional quotation affordance, not a card accent stripe.

**Visual overlays**: Not available in this environment (no user-visible browser); evidence gathered via Playwright screenshots of all 10 routes in light/dark and mobile.

## Priority Issues

1. **[P1] The About page doesn't earn the trust the site depends on.** One paragraph, a stock office photo, and two stats. No partner names, qualifications, ICAI membership/FRN, team, or practice history. Fix: add partner profile(s) with credentials and a short firm-history narrative (ICAI rules permit factual bios).
2. **[P1] A "Hello World" placeholder post is featured on the homepage.** "From the blog" showcases a 1-minute placeholder dated 11 July 2026. Fix: publish one real post (e.g. an NRI property-sale TDS explainer) or hide the section until content exists.
3. **[P1] Careers form: required Gender field, no CV upload.** Gender-required is exclusionary and unnecessary at application stage; and candidates cannot attach a resume (FormSubmit AJAX doesn't take files), so every application arrives without the one document reviewers need. Fix: make gender optional with "Prefer not to say" (or drop it); accept CVs via Netlify Forms file upload (site already deploys on Netlify) or instruct emailing the CV.
4. **[P2] 512 kB single JS bundle + Google Fonts dependency.** react-markdown/remark-gfm ship to every visitor of a brochure site; fonts load from Google's CDN. Fix: lazy-load blog routes via dynamic import; self-host the two font families.
5. **[P2] Disclaimer modal friction and copy mismatch.** It reappears every session (sessionStorage) while its copy claims persistent storage; "I Disagree" ejects the visitor to google.com. Fix: align copy with behavior (or use localStorage if compliance allows), and make Disagree show a polite explanation instead of a hard redirect.

## Persona Red Flags

**Jordan (First-Timer, business owner)**: Hits a legal disclaimer before any content — unavoidable (ICAI), but then finds no named humans anywhere on the site. Can't answer "who will I actually be dealing with?" before calling. No fees/engagement-process guidance anywhere.

**Casey (Distracted Mobile User)**: Mobile layout is solid (stacked CTAs, 44px tap targets, tel: links). But 512 kB JS + external fonts on a 3G connection delays first paint; the office-info panel is hidden on mobile (footer covers it, acceptable).

**Riley (Stress Tester)**: Contact form submits with "a" as name and "1" as subject — only browser-native validation. If the FormSubmit endpoint token is ever stale, the form fails with a generic error; the email fallback saves this. 404 page is genuinely good (three recovery paths).

## Minor Observations

- Careers "Position" select's placeholder option reads "I'm applying for" — label-as-option; use "Select a position".
- Dark mode: hero/footer band sections sit very close to the page background, weakening the light theme's strong section rhythm.
- Success note "Thanks for submitting!" misses a peak-end opportunity — say when they'll hear back.
- `og:image` points at `hero.jpg` (a generic tower) — a logo lockup card would represent the firm better in link previews.
- SEO plumbing (sitemap, robots, RSS, JSON-LD, canonical) is genuinely complete — rare and good.
